# Personas

Use these personas to sanity-check whether a domain or practice serves real users. They are sketches, not market segments.

## End-user personas

### Sam, busy citizen, average digital skills

Wants a quick, correct answer to a benefits question on a phone, during a lunch break. Expects plain language and a clear next step. Will give up if the assistant feels evasive or asks too many questions.

### Rachida, citizen with limited digital skills

Reads at roughly B1 level. Needs short sentences, no jargon, and an obvious path to a human if anything is unclear. Disability or stress may make multi-step interactions hard.

### Erik, small-business owner

Needs to know which regulations apply to a planned activity. Will copy answers into a memo, so citations and sources matter to him as much as the answer itself.

## Internal personas

### Priya, policy officer

Drafts memos and decisions. Uses the assistant to find prior policy, related rulings, and parliamentary documents. Cares about source coverage and recency.

### Tom, service desk agent

Handles citizen calls. Uses an internal assistant to surface the right procedure quickly. Cannot afford a wrong answer; needs the assistant to clearly say when it does not know.

### Lara, product owner

Owns the assistant's roadmap. Decides what to ship, pause, or roll back. Needs evaluation results, incident data, and a clear governance trail.

### Wouter, risk and compliance officer

Signs off on releases that touch personal data or have legal impact. Reads the impact assessment, the algorithm-register entry, and the test results.
